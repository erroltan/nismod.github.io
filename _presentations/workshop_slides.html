Identify key metrics that measure performance of the system [20 mins] In the wider infrastructure debate, [infrastructure performance indicators](http://research.ncl.ac.uk/ibuild/outputs/reports/ICIF&iBUILD%20Indicators%20Interim%20Summary%20Report%20Final.pdf) can be used to monitor: 1. whether infrastructure performance levels are maintained over time; 2. infrastructure performance levels in relation to other countries; 3. the impacts of investment. -- On __red__ PostIt notes, identify and list measurements of performance (metrics) relevant to your sector. -- - Place the metric PostIt notes in the appropriate grid squares on the spatio-temporal grid. -- Together: - __Discuss__ how do performance metrics across the sectors relate to one another? - What is the appropriate temporal and spatial scale at which to measure these metrics? - How could measures of risk be used in the operational or planning problems? --- # 6. Infrastructure assets [20 mins] __Work in pairs with someone from a different sector team.__ Using the draft data schema, identify and list 2 or 3 different examples of infrastructure __assets__ for each of the sectors. -- Pick one asset for each sector in the pair: - What __attributes__ are associated with this asset? - Is the __asset__ associated with an investment decision? - How does the availability of the asset affect system operation? - In what year are the assets available? - How do the asset __attributes__ affect system operation? - What's the most appropriate spatial resolution? - Where are the assets located? --- # 2a. 'planning' versus 'simulation' [20 mins] __The planning problem__ - in each [decade/year] - what to build (assets), where and when - how much does it cost? - by how much does it improve performance? __The simulation problem__ - within each year - how does the system perform? - how much does it cost to operate? - does the system meet demand? --- # 2b. 'planning' versus 'simulation' [20 mins] - is there any investment logic in the sector model? - does any part of the simulation not fit within a year? - Think in terms of _state_ - what information is required on the 1st January which is computed on the 31st December? e.g. reservoir level - does your model compute costs for operating? - Are these important for the planning problem? e.g. capital cost intensive renewables versus operational cost intensive coal plants? --- # 3. Strategic Investment Rules [20 mins] ## What is the relationship between operation of the sector and planning in the sector? __Write a rule__ which relates an output or metric from the model to a decision to invest. This could be the form of an if-else-then statement, or a graph showing a threshold or relationship between an output and investment. --- # 4. Key decision variables [20 mins] _A decision variable is a parameter that is associated with an investment (or any other kind of) decision, whose value is determined by the strategic logic layer._ The value of a decision variable can be - pre-specified - specified by planning rules - or automatically determined by the optimiser Some examples: - build a 1 GW nuclear power station in location _x_ in 2017 - reduce residential water demand (through free water widgets) by 6Ml/day Identify three different targets for decision variables in your sector model with some examples. --- # 5. Inputs Required Identify three sector model inputs which are produced by - another sector model (a dependency) - a socio-economic scenario - an improvement in technology
