Infrastructure assets [20 mins] ## Work in pairs with someone from a different sector team Using the draft data schema, identify and list 2 or 3 different examples of infrastructure __assets__ for each of the sectors. -- ## Pick one asset for each sector in the pair: - What __attributes__ are associated with this asset? - Is the __asset__ associated with an investment decision? - How does the availability of the asset affect system operation? - In what year are the assets available? - How do the asset __attributes__ affect system operation? - What's the most appropriate spatial resolution? - Where are the assets located? --- # 2.2a. 'planning' versus 'simulation' [20 mins] ## __The planning problem__ - in each [decade/year] - what to build (assets), where and when - how much does it cost? - by how much does it improve performance? ## __The simulation problem__ - within each year - how does the system perform? - how much does it cost to operate? - does the system meet demand? --- # 2.2b. 'planning' versus 'simulation' [20 mins] ## Discuss in pairs - is there any investment logic in the sector model? - does any part of the simulation not fit within a year? - Think in terms of _state_ - what information is required on the 1st January which is computed on the 31st December? e.g. reservoir level - does your model compute costs for operating? - Are these important for the planning problem? e.g. capital cost intensive renewables versus operational cost intensive coal plants? --- # 2.3. Strategic Investment Rules [20 mins] ## What is the relationship between operation of the sector and planning of the sector? __Write a rule__ which relates an output or metric from the model to a decision to invest. This could be the form of an if-else-then statement, or a graph showing a threshold or relationship between an output and investment. --- # 2.4. Key decision variables [20 mins] _A decision variable is a parameter that is associated with an investment (or any other kind of) decision, whose value is determined by the strategic logic layer._ The value of a decision variable can be - pre-specified - specified by planning rules - automatically determined by the optimiser Some examples: - build a 1 GW nuclear power station in location _x_ in 2017 - reduce residential water demand (through free water widgets) by 6Ml/day in catchment _y_ in 2023 ## Identify three different targets for decision variables in your sector model with some examples. --- # 2.5. Inputs Required ## Identify three sector model inputs which are produced by - another sector model (a dependency) - a price, result or metric - provision of an infrastructure service - a socio-economic scenario attribute - a change in technology
